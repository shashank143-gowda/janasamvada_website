How It Works

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about reporting corruption

Yes, your identity is completely protected. All reports are anonymous by default, and we do not collect any personally identifiable information unless you choose to provide it. Our system is designed to ensure whistleblower protection.

After submission, your report goes through a verification process by our team. Once verified, it is forwarded to the appropriate authorities for investigation. You will receive a unique tracking ID to check the status of your report.

Any evidence that supports your claim is helpful, such as photos, videos, audio recordings, documents, receipts, or written communications. However, reports without evidence are also accepted and investigated based on the information provided.

The investigation timeline varies depending on the complexity of the case, the department involved, and the available evidence. Simple cases may be resolved within a few weeks, while complex cases might take several months. You can always check the status using your tracking ID.

Yes, you can report corruption incidents that you have witnessed or that have been shared with you by others. The most important aspect is providing accurate and detailed information about the incident to facilitate proper investigation.
