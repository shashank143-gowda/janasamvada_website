Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about reporting corruption and how the anti-corruption portal works.

Yes, your identity is completely protected. Our system uses end-to-end encryption to secure your information. You can choose to report anonymously without providing any personal details, or you can provide contact information that will be kept confidential and only used if additional information is needed for the investigation.

After submission, your report is assigned a unique ID and forwarded to the relevant anti-corruption unit. The authorities will review your report, gather additional evidence if needed, and initiate an investigation. You can track the status of your report using the ID provided. Depending on the complexity, investigations may take a few weeks to several months to complete.

Any evidence that supports your corruption claim is valuable. This could include photos, videos, audio recordings, documents, receipts, names of witnesses, dates, times, and detailed descriptions of the incidents. The more specific and detailed your evidence, the easier it will be for investigators to verify and act on your report.

Yes, you can report past incidents of corruption. While recent cases may be easier to investigate, historical cases are still important to report, especially if they involve ongoing patterns of corruption or significant amounts. There may be statutory limitations for certain types of corruption, but the authorities will determine this during their assessment.

In some cases, whistleblowers may be eligible for rewards, especially if the report leads to significant recovery of public funds or prevention of major fraud. However, the primary purpose of reporting corruption should be to contribute to a more transparent and honest society. Any potential rewards are determined by the relevant authorities based on applicable laws and the specifics of each case.
